Step 01

Welcome

Start of the experience

Begin the pamphlet with a warm introduction that sets the tone for the evening. This section should quickly tell guests what the event is, who it honors, and why the night matters.

Use this as your digital front cover: event title, mood, date, location, and one short line that feels celebratory and polished.

Include

Event title, welcome line, date, time, and location.

Visual vibe

Clean hero section with strong typography and soft sketch-style background details.

Step 02

Overview

Evening flow

This part explains the structure of the event so guests know what to expect. Think of it as the table of contents for the night.

You can list the major moments such as welcome, program highlights, recognition, closing, and any networking or expo pieces.

Suggested bullets

Welcome • Program • Senior Recognition • Closing

Best use

Keep it short and scannable so it feels like a real pamphlet spread.

Step 03

Seniors

Recognition section

Spotlight the graduating seniors here. This section can feature names, short accomplishments, highlights, future plans, or a concise sentence that celebrates their work and impact.

If you want this to feel interactive, each senior can become a clickable card later.

Can hold

Name, concentration, favorite project, internship, or post-grad plan.

Layout idea

Grid of cards or spotlight profiles that open into more detail.

Step 04

Next Event

Bridge forward

Use this area to point guests toward what comes after this event. It works perfectly for something like the Senior Expo or any upcoming ITD gathering.

Keep it clear: event name, date, time, place, and one sentence about why guests should attend.

Suggested content

Senior Expo — date, time, location, and why it matters.

Optional add-on

Add a button, QR code, or RSVP link later.

Step 05

About ITD

Program identity

End the pamphlet by grounding the event in the ITD program itself. This section should reinforce what ITD stands for: creativity, innovation, design thinking, and interdisciplinary work.

It gives the whole experience context and leaves guests with a stronger understanding of the program behind the celebration.

Can include

Mission, disciplines, student outcomes, and design-centered values.

Ending note

Close with a polished sentence about the future of ITD and its students.
